Dialysis Centre

During the course of running our Medical Aid program, KCT Trust members would come across numerous cases in need of urgent dialysis treatment. Since dialysis treatment is an on-going cost, for the underprivileged, it involved a formidable cost that they can ill-afford. In 2016, with the guidance and expert advice of doctors, KCT Trustees and mentors decided to set-up a well-equipped dialysis centre. The centre at XXXXX, takes an all-inclusive approach in which the medicines are also allotted to the enrolled, critically-ill patients.

Reality Check

Yearly, around 2,20,000 patients in India are diagnosed with end-stage renal disease (ESRD). Every year, there is an added chunk of 34 million dialysis sessions added to the existing pool of patients. Nearly 70% of this target population of patients are not able to afford or continue with the recurring treatment due to fiscal limitations or untimely death. Only miniscule 10–20% patients in India receive dialysis on time and are able to go on with the lengthy treatment.

KCT Remedy

The KCT Trust’s first measure was to spread more awareness in our society about the urgent need to increase capacity for dialysis procedure. Since this was not available with the government hospitals and private hospitals were out of bounds for poor patients, KCT Trustees eventually decided to set-up their own centre for provision of regular dialysis sessions. This Centre also distributes medicines, gives injections and provides for the patients’ ration needs through our different programs. At the centre, we have a team that helps them understand how they can avail which government schemes to lessen their disease burden with financial aid.
